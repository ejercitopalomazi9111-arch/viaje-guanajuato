    Women in STEAM

    The minds
    behind the numbers.

    STEAM is not only about places and tools — it is about the people who made science possible. This short film honours Katherine Johnson and the women who fought their way into laboratories, agencies, and history.

    1918 — 2020 · NASA

    Katherine Johnson

    Katherine Johnson was an African-American mathematician at NASA whose hand-calculated orbital trajectories made human spaceflight possible. She computed the path for the first American in space and verified the numbers for John Glenn's orbit around the Earth — by his own request, because he trusted her maths over the new computers.

    She did it while facing the barriers of racial segregation and a field that did not expect women. Her story — told in Hidden Figures — is why this expedition closes with her: the A in STEAM is also for the courage it takes to belong where you were told you could not.
